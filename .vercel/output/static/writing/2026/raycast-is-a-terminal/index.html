Raycast is a terminal

~/writing/2026/raycast-is-a-terminal May 2026
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Raycast is a terminal for normies who don’t know what a zsh is. Raycast and terminals work the same: you type a command, you get (mostly) textual output. That’s it.

In a traditional CLI, you type something, and you get text back — whether that’s a file listing, an error, or the slow, creeping, inescapable horror of accidentally opening vim.

Raycast does the same thing, but with a nicer output layer. Instead of raw stdout, you get a List, a Grid, some nicely rendered Markdown, or a Form. Strip away the polish and those are just: options as a list, options as a table, text out, text in. Every UI primitive Raycast offers maps directly to a terminal concept.

If you still don’t believe me, Raycast supports bash scripts as commands. So it’s not like a terminal, it is a terminal. Just a terminal for people who don’t use terminals. A hotkey away, always. Nice enough that your “WhaT’S a FiLE” friends won’t flee in terror.

That’s Raycast. Or is it a terminal? Why not both? They’re the same thing anyways.
